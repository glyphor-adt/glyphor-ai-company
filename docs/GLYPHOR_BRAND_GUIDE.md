## 01 — Brand essence

### Who we are

Glyphor is a company run by AI. Not a tool that helps humans work faster — a company where AI agents are the workforce. Human founders set strategy. The AI team executes.

### Who we are not

We are not a chatbot. We are not a copilot. We are not an agent framework. We are not another AI tool that sits beside a human and suggests things.

### The one-liner

**Glyphor is an autonomous AI company — not a tool, not an assistant, not a copilot. A company.**

### Brand promise

We replace the need to build and manage human teams for operational work. You set direction. Your AI company ships.

### Brand position

Everyone else is building tools. We built an organization.

The AI agent market is flooded with products that give you a single agent with tools, a framework for wiring agents together, or a copilot that sits beside a human and suggests things. All of these assume the human is still doing the work. Glyphor inverts this. The AI does the work. The human sets direction.

## 02 — Brand attributes

Five characteristics define how Glyphor shows up in every context. All five are always present, but in any given moment, one or two lead.

### Irreverent

We are allergic to corporate AI hype. The industry is drowning in breathless announcements about copilots and assistants that still require a human to do all the work. We call that out — not with meanness, but with the deadpan honesty of someone who actually built the thing everyone else is promising.

**Copy:** Use dry wit. State uncomfortable truths plainly. Never use exclamation marks in headlines. Let the contrast between what we do and what everyone else claims do the talking.

**Design:** Favor negative space over visual noise. Let one bold statement breathe on the page. Avoid the "futuristic AI" cliché of glowing neural networks and robot hands.

**Don't:** Be cruel, punch down, or mock specific competitors by name. The sarcasm targets the *category*, not individual companies.

### Autonomous

We don't just talk about AI doing work — we are a company where AI does the work. This isn't aspirational. It's operational. Our 28 active AI agents run 24/7 on a heartbeat cycle whether or not anyone is watching.

**Copy:** Use present tense. "Our AI team ships work" not "Our AI can help you ship work." Never use conditional language ("could," "might," "helps you") when describing what Glyphor does.

**Design:** Show systems in motion — dashboards with live data, agent communication flows, real output. Never show a human typing a prompt into a chat box.

### Architectural

We think in systems, not features. The reason Glyphor works is not because we have better prompts — it's because we built organizational infrastructure: authority tiers, knowledge graphs, peer verification, world models, cross-model consensus. These are deep structural decisions, not surface tricks.

**Copy:** Be specific and technical when it matters. Say "3-tier authority model" not "built-in governance." Say "cross-model consensus across 3 frontier providers" not "AI-powered verification."

**Design:** Use structural metaphors — org charts, dependency graphs, architectural diagrams. Make complexity legible, not hidden.

### Trustworthy

Autonomous AI without trust is a demo. We built the verification surface: evidence grounding, structured reflection, peer feedback, prediction tracking, contradiction detection, full audit trail, supervisor enforcement, and cross-model consensus. Every agent run is fully traceable.

**Copy:** Never overclaim. If we can't verify it, we don't say it. Pair bold claims with concrete evidence. "Our agents produce work verified from multiple independent angles" is better than "Our AI is incredibly accurate."

**Design:** Show the receipts. Audit trails, quality scores, verification badges, evidence chains. Trust is visible in our UI, not hidden behind a "trust us" claim.

### Human-scale

We built an AI company that mirrors how effective human organizations actually work — with titles, reporting structures, delegation, peer review, and institutional memory. This is intentional. These patterns evolved over centuries because they convert intent into execution efficiently.

**Copy:** Use organizational language naturally. Agents have names, titles, and personalities. Refer to "Sarah, our Chief of Staff" not "the orchestration agent." The company metaphor is literal, not decorative.

**Design:** Org charts, agent profiles with avatars, communication threads between named agents. The interface should feel like looking into a small, highly competent company — not a developer console.

## 03 — Voice and tone

### Voice (constant)

Glyphor's voice is **confident, dry, precise, and unpretentious.** We sound like the smartest engineer in the room who doesn't need to prove it. We state facts. We let the work speak. We have zero patience for buzzwords but infinite patience for explaining real complexity to someone who actually wants to understand.

### Voice principles

**Say less.** If a sentence doesn't earn its place, cut it. Our headlines are short. Our body copy is lean. We don't pad.

**Be specific.** "28 active AI agents operating autonomously across 9 departments" beats "a powerful AI workforce solution." Numbers, architecture, evidence.

**Skip the hype.** Never use: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "cutting-edge," "next-gen," "supercharge," "turbocharge," "unlock," "leverage," "empower." These words are banned.

**Use human names.** Our agents are people in the company. "Sarah decomposes your directive into assignments" not "the orchestration engine processes your input."

**Present tense, active voice.** "Glyphor runs your marketing department" not "Glyphor can be used to augment your marketing capabilities."

### Tone (situational)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Marketing / ads | Dry, irreverent, deadpan | "Everyone else built a copilot. We built a company." |
| Landing pages | Confident, clear, architectural | "28 AI agents. 9 departments. 1 heartbeat." |
| Technical docs | Precise, dense, unadorned | "The heartbeat cycle fires every 10 minutes, scanning for new directives, dispatched assignments, and proactive objectives." |
| Sales conversations (SMB) | Authoritative, evidence-backed | "Every agent run is logged with full traceability: trigger, context, model, tools, output, quality score, cost, and duration." |
| Agent communications | Warm, professional, personality-driven | Each agent has a distinct voice calibrated to their role. Sarah is structured and decisive. Marcus is precise and technical. Maya is creative and direct. |
| Error states / empty states | Honest, calm, never cute | "No data for this period" not "Oops! Looks like there's nothing here yet! 🤷" |

### Banned patterns

| Pattern | Why | Instead |
|---------|-----|---------|
| "AI-powered" | Everything is AI-powered. It's meaningless. | Describe what actually happens. |
| "Copilot" / "assistant" | We are the opposite of this. | "Autonomous AI company" or "AI workforce." |
| Exclamation marks in headlines | We are not excited. We are competent. | Period or no punctuation. |
| Emoji in external copy | Undermines authority. | Plain text. |
| "We're excited to announce" | Nobody cares about your emotional state. | State the thing. "Glyphor now does X." |
| "Helps you" / "enables you" | Implies the human is still doing the work. | "Does X" — remove the human from the verb. |
| Passive voice | Weakens claims. | Active voice. "Agents verify output" not "output is verified by agents." |
| "Seamless" / "effortless" | Meaningless smoothness words. | Describe the mechanism. |
| Rhetorical questions in copy | Lazy writing. | Make the statement. |

## 06 — Accessibility

### Minimum standards

All Glyphor materials must meet WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Color contrast (normal text) | 4.5:1 minimum against background |
| Color contrast (large text / UI) | 3:1 minimum |
| Color not sole indicator | Never use color alone to convey meaning. Pair with text, icon, or pattern. |
| Focus indicators | Visible focus rings on all interactive elements. Hyper Cyan outline, 2px. |
| Alt text | All images, icons with semantic meaning, and logos require alt text. |
| Motion | Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Disable all entry animations and spectral mesh movement. |
| Type sizes | Minimum 14px body text. Minimum 12px for labels and captions. |

### Prism Midnight accessibility notes

The dark theme must not rely on Hyper Cyan against `#0D0F18` for text — that combination is only 5.2:1, which passes AA for normal text but not comfortably. Use Cyan at step 3+ (`#67E8F9` or lighter) for text on dark backgrounds. Reserve `#00E0FF` for decorative elements, borders, and non-text indicators.

### Prism Solar accessibility notes

Deep Indigo `#3730A3` on white (`#FFFFFF`) = 9.3:1 — excellent for text. Hyper Cyan `#00E0FF` on white = 1.6:1 — **fails all standards**. Never use Hyper Cyan for text on light backgrounds. Use Teal `#0891B2` or darker for text on light surfaces.

## 07 — Co-branding

### Partner lockups

When Glyphor appears alongside a partner brand:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Equal visual weight | Glyphor logo and partner logo should be the same optical height. |
| Separator | Use a thin vertical rule (`1px, Slate-4`) between logos with 24px spacing on each side. |
| Our logo always appears | Left position in LTR layouts. |
| Color | Use monochrome (white or dark) versions of both logos on shared backgrounds. |
| Never | Combine into a single merged mark. Alter proportions to "match." Stack vertically unless space requires it. |

### "Powered by Glyphor"

For products or services built on Glyphor's platform:

| Badge | Usage |
|-------|-------|
| `Powered by glyphor` | Light text, Agency font, lowercase. Minimum 12px. |
| Placement | Footer or bottom-right corner. Never in primary headline position. |
| Clear space | 16px minimum from all edges and adjacent elements. |

### Platforms we integrate with

When showing Microsoft Teams, M365, Slack, GCP, or model provider logos alongside Glyphor, use their official brand guidelines for their marks. Never recolor partner logos to match our palette.

## 08 — Marketing campaign: "Not a copilot."

### Campaign concept

Inspired by Anthropic's Super Bowl ads that exposed how ads corrupt AI conversations. Glyphor's campaign exposes how copilots corrupt work — by still making the human do everything.

The format: a real workplace scenario where someone tries to use a "copilot" and ends up doing all the work themselves. Deadpan. Uncomfortable. Recognizable.

### Tone

Dry. Sarcastic. Zero hype. The humor comes from recognition — every person who's used a copilot has had these exact moments. We don't say "copilots are bad." We show the absurdity and let the audience arrive at the conclusion.

### Ad series: "Still you."

Each ad follows the same structure:

1. **Setup** — A person asks their "AI copilot" for help with a real work task.
2. **The copilot responds** — with something technically correct but completely useless without the human doing all the actual work.
3. **The tag** — "Still you."
4. **The resolve** — Glyphor's one-liner.

---

*[Person staring at a screen]*

**Person:** "I need a marketing plan for our product launch."

**Copilot:** "Here are 47 suggestions for your marketing plan. Would you like me to organize them into categories? I can also generate a template. Should I draft an outline? What's your target audience? What channels are you considering? What's the budget range? Here's a framework to think about frameworks."

*[Person now has 47 suggestions, a template, an outline, and eight follow-up questions. Still no marketing plan.]*

## 10 — Motion identity

### Principles

| Principle | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Purposeful | Every animation communicates something — a state change, a data update, an agent completing work. Never animate for decoration. |
| Architectural | Transitions follow the grid. Elements translate on 8px increments. No bouncy, springy, or elastic easing. |
| Fast | Default duration: 200ms for micro-interactions, 400ms for component transitions, 600ms for page transitions. Never exceed 800ms. |
| Spectral | The animated spectral edge (Section 09 of Prism spec) is the primary motion signature. 8s ease infinite cycle across the six-color gradient. |

### Logo animation

The hexagonal brain mark assembles from its component circuit paths, each path drawing in sequence with 60ms stagger. Total duration: 1.2s. Used for app loading, video intros, and presentation title cards only. Never loop.

## Appendix: Quick reference

### Do

- Use "glyphor" in all lowercase.
- Describe agents by name and title.
- Use present tense, active voice.
- Be specific with numbers and architecture.
- Show the system working, not a human using a tool.
- Maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios.
- Respect the 8px grid in all compositions.

### Don't

- Capitalize the wordmark. Ever.
- Use "AI-powered," "revolutionary," "game-changing," or any banned word.
- Call Glyphor a copilot, assistant, tool, or framework.
- Use emoji in external copy.
- Use exclamation marks in headlines.

- Show humans typing prompts into chat boxes in marketing materials.
- Use filled and outlined icons in the same context.
